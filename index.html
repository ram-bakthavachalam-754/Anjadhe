This Mac can't run the AI on its own

This Mac doesn’t have enough memory to run the AI by itself. Two ways to add a brain later in Settings › AI Assistant: run the model on another computer you own — a desktop or a home server — or bring your own OpenAI or Anthropic API key, if that’s a service you trust with what you send it. Where should your data live?

By default everything stays in a private folder on this Mac. Use more than one Mac? Keep your data in a folder that syncs (like iCloud Drive or Dropbox) so all your Macs stay up to date. No account needed, and it stays encrypted.

You can change this anytime in Settings › Storage.

Build Apps

Build your own apps

Build apps for yourself with any coding agent. Apps you build appear under “Your Apps” on your home page, and their data is stored locally, backed up, and synced across your Macs — automatically.

Point your coding agent at this folder — it reads the included AGENTS.md and builds apps right here. Changes hot-reload while Anjadhe is running; load errors are written to each app’s .errors.log so the agent can fix its own mistakes.

Builds run on the AI Assistant’s model — one brain for chat and building. Change it in the assistant’s model picker.

App Studio

Build apps by describing them to the AI, right inside Anjadhe. Experimental and hidden by default — turning it on adds App Studio to the launcher and lets the assistant build and edit apps.

    About

    A personal assistant
    that lives with your data

    Cloud-based AI chat tools are great — until the question involves personal or confidential data you don't want to share. If you're not comfortable handing your most personal information — your inbox, your money, your health, your family — to tools running on someone else's servers, Anjadhe is for you.

    Your personal data — email, calendar, tasks, notes, journal — lives on your Mac, laid out in Anjadhe's own apps where you can see and work with it yourself, and the AI comes to where the data is. It's the assistant you can hand your whole picture to — because by default, nothing about your life leaves your machine. There's no account and no sign-up: connect Gmail and your email comes straight from Google to this Mac.

    Anjadhe is a canvas: we've built a set of useful apps on it for you, and shipped a personal AI assistant that helps you stay organized, plan your goals, create documents, and answer your questions — or even build more apps, using any coding agent.

    AI that works for you, privately

    On a powerful Mac — 32 GB of memory or more — the assistant runs completely on private, local AI: a free model you download once, and everything stays on your machine. On a lighter Mac, you choose where the AI runs instead: bring your own key from a provider you trust, like OpenAI or Anthropic, or host the model on another server you trust. Your data goes only where you point it.

    Actions: open it, and it tells you what to do today

    Focus, goals, and daily actions are altitudes of one question — what am I doing with my time? Anjadhe keeps them connected behind a single Actions door with just two tabs. Tasks is the front door — Today's short, finishable list, with Tomorrow, This Week, This Month, Later, and each focus area one click away — and Plan is where focus areas hold goals, broken into tasks by hand or by the assistant. Repeating tasks build the streaks that compound underneath — and every small action still knows why it exists.
